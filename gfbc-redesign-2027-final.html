About Our Church

Who We Are

A Community of Faith
in the Heart of Washington, DC

The Greater First Baptist Church of Mount Pleasant Plains is a historic congregation in the heart of Washington, DC. Founded in 1876 by emancipated slaves who gathered for song and prayer, our church has served this community for nearly 150 years.

We are a diverse family of believers united by a common faith in Jesus Christ and a shared mission to love God, love others, and magnify His name in all we do. Our legacy includes dedicated pastoral leadership from founding pastor Rev. Andrew Green through Dr. Winston C. Ridley and now Rev. Dr. Jonathan Z. Queen.

Foundation and Ministry

The Greater First Baptist Church is grounded in a strong tradition of faith, education, and service. From its earliest days, the church has emphasized sound biblical teaching, spiritual formation, and equipping believers to serve within the church and throughout the broader community.

Throughout its history, the church has been shaped by dedicated pastoral and ministerial leadership. This journey reflects a balance of tradition and growth — honoring foundational values while responding to the evolving needs of the congregation and community.

The church actively engages with community organizations, faith-based councils, and service initiatives that promote collaboration, compassion, and positive impact. Through these partnerships, Greater First Baptist continues to serve as a trusted presence in the Mount Pleasant Plains neighborhood.

Our History

A Legacy of Faith

150 Years of Faithful Service

From humble beginnings in 1876 to the sanctuary we worship in today, The Greater First Baptist Church has been a cornerstone of faith, hope, and service in the Mount Pleasant Plains community of Washington, DC for nearly a century and a half.

"Our prayer is that this the Greater First Baptist Church, Mount Pleasant Plains, will stand as a bulwark of faith in years to come as it has stood the years past; a living monument of our Saviour who died that all men might live."
From our 1978 Centennial History
1876

The Beginning

A few emancipated slaves, feeling the need of religious guidance, met in the home of sister Charlotte Hamilton on Sherman Avenue, north of Kenyon Street, Mount Pleasant Plains, and started a song and prayer class. Founding deacons Jerry Sykes and Samuel Lee led the early congregation.

1877–78

First Baptist Church, Mt. Pleasant Plains

They built a frame mission on the corner of Sherman Avenue and Kenyon Street, given the name "First Baptist Church, Mount Pleasant Plains" in 1878. Rev. Andrew Green served as pastor and founder.

1890s

Faith & Perseverance

Two lots on the southeast corner of Lamont Street and Sherman Avenue were purchased. Facing a foreclosure mortgage of $500, it was the sisters of the church who saved it — Sister Betty Barnes secured a $5,500 loan from Mr. Cook on September 25, 1896.

1910–11

A New Building

Rev. Daniel Washington of King George, Virginia was called as pastor in April 1910. The old frame building was torn down and services held in a tent until the brick church building was completed in December 1911.

1923

Mortgage Paid in Full

After years of faithful sacrifice by the entire congregation, the mortgage was paid in full and a clear title obtained. Rev. Daniel Washington served faithfully for 33 years, retiring in 1939.

1947

Rev. Edward Thomas, D.D.

Installed May 25, 1947, Rev. Thomas led the church through continuous growth — spiritually, numerically, and financially. The church purchased "The Fourth Presbyterian Church and Parsonage" at 13th and Fairmount Streets for $400,000.

Mid-20th C.

The Greater First Baptist Church

Under Rev. Thomas, the church was reincorporated under its current name, "The Greater First Baptist Church, Mount Pleasant Plains," with the congregation pledging renewed dedication to God and ministry.

1978

Jubilee Centennial Celebration

The church celebrated its Jubilee Centennial with a historic mortgage-burning ceremony in Rev. Thomas's 31st year as pastor.

2001

Dr. Winston C. Ridley, Jr.

Following an election on January 27, 2001, Dr. Winston C. Ridley preached his first sermon as Pastor-Elect on March 4, 2001, and was installed May 20, 2001. His leadership brought a new season of vision, stability, and service as the church continued its witness in a changing city. Dr. Ridley also conceived the FOCUS Newsletter Ministry in 2003.

2026

Rev. Dr. Jonathan Z. Queen

In January 2026, the church entered a new chapter with the election of Rev. Dr. Jonathan Z. Queen as Pastor. Installed March 28, 2026, Dr. Queen brings a vision grounded in unity, love, and shared purpose — honoring the legacy of those who came before while embracing God's call for the future.

1876–Today

A Living Testimony

From 1876 to today — nearly 150 years — The Greater First Baptist Church of Washington, DC stands as a living testimony: a people shaped by prayer, strengthened by perseverance, and sustained by the faithfulness of God.

Be Part of Our Continuing Story

Join us as we continue the legacy of faith, service, and community that began in 1876.

Our Leadership

Committed to the Cause

Shepherds Under Christ

Our leadership team recognizes the authority of God, and realizes that we are shepherds who serve under the Chief Shepherd, Jesus Christ. We desire to devote ourselves to prayer and to the ministry of the Word. "Equipping the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ."
Ephesians 4:12
Senior Pastor
Rev. Dr. Jonathan Z. Queen

Dr. Queen received his call to Gospel ministry in March 2009 and preached his trial sermon on August 22, 2009. He holds a B.S. in Bible and Christian Ministry from Lancaster Bible College (2011), an M.Div. from Lancaster Theological Seminary (2015), and a Doctor of Ministry from Regent University (2024), where his dissertation focused on a church-based reentry program for formerly incarcerated men.

Before coming to Greater First Baptist, Dr. Queen served as Associate Pastor and Youth Pastor at First Baptist Church of Glenarden International under Senior Pastor John K. Jenkins, Sr. He has proclaimed the Gospel across the United States and internationally in South Africa, Rwanda, Peru, and the Holy Land. He is an award-winning author of six books, a motivational speaker, and expert in mentoring and reentry. Life Verses: 1 Timothy 3:1-7; Romans 12:1-2; 2 Timothy 2:24-26.

First Lady · Licensed & Ordained Minister
First Lady Rev. Lenia Queen

First Lady Rev. Lenia Queen is the devoted wife of Rev. Dr. Jonathan Z. Queen and the proud mother of six adult children ("Mom-Mom" to nine grandchildren). She holds an A.A. in Human Resource Management from Virginia College, a Professional HR Certificate from Villanova University, a B.S. in Bible from Lancaster Bible College, and an M.A. in Clinical Mental Health Counseling from LBC | Capital Seminary & Graduate School. She is a nationally board-certified and licensed therapist.

She accepted Christ in 1986, rededicated her life in 2001, was licensed as a minister in October 2021, and ordained in August 2024 at First Baptist Church of Glenarden under Senior Pastor John K. Jenkins, Sr. She served as Associate Pastor and group ministry coordinator for Children and Youth at First Baptist Church of Glenarden.

She is the CEO, founder, and lead clinician of Sister2Sister Therapy. Alongside her husband, she co-founded New Mindz Ministries, Inc., a 501(c)(3) providing mentoring, mental health support, and reentry resources for at-risk youth, adults, and returning citizens. Her belief: "Healed hearts build healthy families and healthy families build strong churches and communities."

Our Ministries

You Can Make a Difference

A Place for Every Season of Life

Our church is not a place for spectators — it is a place for team members. Whether you are a five-year-old child, a ninety-five-year-old grandmother, a busy mom, or a businessperson with big responsibilities, we want you to be part of our vibrant community.

All weekly sermons are uploaded in video and audio formats. We maintain an active Facebook and YouTube feed for additional church communication and outreach.

Small Group Bible Study

Connect with others in fellowship, grow in biblical understanding, meet the needs of others in ministry, and nurture your relationship with God. Groups meet throughout the week.

🚌
Youth Ministry

Each Sunday morning, children participate in their own classes where Bible lessons are taught so that every child gets to participate, ask questions, and learn at their level.

👩‍👩‍👧
Women's Group

Women's groups provide special times of discipleship, interaction, and refreshing fellowship — a space for the ladies of our congregation to grow and be encouraged in faith.

Vacation Bible School

VBS is a ministry outreach to educate children about God. Church membership is not required. An inviting atmosphere for kids and families to connect with our church community.

Music & Drama

Instrumentalists, vocalists, choir members, and drama participants use their talents and gifts to glorify God. These volunteers are essential to our worship ministry each Sunday.

🙏
Prayer Warriors

Our Prayer Warriors intercede each Sunday during the service and throughout the week for the pastor, church members, special requests, and missionary needs. Wednesday: corporate prayer and Bible study.

💍

Trained counselor-couples listen, understand, help, and encourage struggling marriages. By God's grace, this ministry has saved marriages and repaired relationships across our congregation.

Dedicated to making disciples throughout all nations through missionary activity and support, fulfilling the Great Commission to spread the Gospel from DC to the ends of the earth.
